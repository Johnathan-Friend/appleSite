About

Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. As of March 2023, Apple is the world's largest company by market capitalization, and with US$394.3 billion the largest technology company by 2022 revenue. As of June 2022, Apple is the fourth-largest personal computer vendor by unit sales; the largest manufacturing company by revenue; and the second-largest mobile phone manufacturer in the world. It is considered one of the Big Five American information technology companies, alongside Alphabet (parent company of Google), Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft.

Apple Inc. is a technology company known for its innovation in consumer electronics, software, and services. It was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Cupertino, California. The company's first product was the Apple I, a computer designed and hand-built by Steve Wozniak. In 1977, the Apple II was released, becoming a major success and establishing Apple as a significant player in the emerging personal computer market.

In 1984, Apple launched the Macintosh, featuring a graphical user interface and a mouse, revolutionizing personal computing. However, internal conflicts led to Steve Jobs leaving Apple in 1985. Throughout the '90s, Apple faced challenges with market share and product success. In 1997, Steve Jobs returned to Apple and initiated a remarkable turnaround. Under his leadership, the company introduced iconic products like the iMac, iPod, iTunes, iPhone, and iPad.

The iPod, released in 2001, transformed the music industry with its portable digital music player and the iTunes Store, while the iPhone, launched in 2007, revolutionized smartphones and changed how people interacted with mobile technology. Apple's focus on design, user experience, and an integrated ecosystem of hardware, software, and services has been central to its success. The company expanded its offerings with services like the App Store, iCloud, Apple Music, and Apple TV+. In 2022, Apple continues to innovate with new products, services, and initiatives, including advancements in artificial intelligence, augmented reality, health technology, and environmental sustainability.
